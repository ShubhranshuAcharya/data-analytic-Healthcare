Patient Education Center

Evidence-based educational resources for diabetes prevention and management

Early Detection

Learn about the importance of regular screenings and early detection of diabetes risk factors.

50% reduction in complications with early detection

Weight Management

Maintain a healthy weight to significantly reduce your risk of developing type 2 diabetes.

58% risk reduction with 7% weight loss

Regular Exercise

Physical activity improves insulin sensitivity and helps maintain healthy blood sugar levels.

150 minutes/week recommended

High Blood Glucose

Fasting glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL or random glucose ≥ 200 mg/dL

Obesity (BMI ≥ 30)

Excess weight increases insulin resistance

Age ≥ 45 years

Risk increases with age, especially after 45

Family History

Having a parent or sibling with diabetes

Physical Activity Goals

  • 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week
  • Strength training exercises 2+ days per week
  • Break up sitting time every 30 minutes

Sleep & Stress Management

  • 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night
  • Practice stress reduction techniques
  • Maintain regular sleep schedule

Healthy Plate Model

½ Vegetables
¼ Protein
¼ Grains

Key Nutrition Tips

  • Choose whole grains over refined carbohydrates
  • Include fiber-rich foods in every meal
  • Limit sugary drinks and processed foods
  • Practice portion control

Screening Schedule

Adults 35-70: Screen every 3 years if BMI ≥ 25
High Risk: Screen annually
Prediabetes: Screen annually

Screening Tests

Fasting Glucose: 8-hour fast required
HbA1c: 3-month average, no fasting needed
Oral Glucose Tolerance: 2-hour test
