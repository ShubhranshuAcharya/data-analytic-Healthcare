Patient Education Center

Evidence-based educational resources for diabetes prevention and management

Early Detection

Regular screenings are crucial for preventing diabetes complications. Early detection through blood glucose testing, HbA1c measurements, and risk factor assessment can reduce complications by up to 50%.

  • Annual screening for adults over 35
  • Every 3 years for lower risk individuals
  • More frequent if risk factors present
50% Reduction in complications with early detection

Weight Management

Maintaining a healthy weight is one of the most effective ways to prevent type 2 diabetes. Even modest weight loss can dramatically reduce your risk.

  • 7% weight loss reduces risk by 58%
  • BMI target: 18.5-24.9 for optimal health
  • Waist circumference: <40" men, <35" women
  • Gradual approach: 1-2 pounds per week
58% Risk reduction with 7% weight loss

Regular Exercise

Physical activity improves insulin sensitivity, helps maintain healthy blood sugar levels, and reduces diabetes risk significantly.

  • 150 minutes/week moderate aerobic activity
  • 2 days/week resistance training
  • Break up sitting time every 30 minutes
  • Start gradually and build intensity
150 Minutes per week recommended

Healthy Diet

A balanced, nutritious diet plays a crucial role in diabetes prevention and blood sugar management.

  • Whole grains instead of refined carbs
  • Lean proteins: fish, poultry, legumes
  • Healthy fats: nuts, olive oil, avocado
  • Limit sugar and processed foods
  • Portion control and meal timing
30% Risk reduction with healthy diet

Understanding Diabetes Risk Factors

Knowledge of risk factors empowers you to take preventive action. Some factors can be modified through lifestyle changes, while others are genetic or age-related.

Modifiable Risk Factors

Obesity (BMI ≥30)

Excess weight, especially around the waist, increases insulin resistance.

High Impact
Physical Inactivity

Sedentary lifestyle reduces glucose uptake by muscles and increases insulin resistance.

High Impact
Smoking

Tobacco use increases diabetes risk by 30-40% and worsens complications.

Medium Impact
Excessive Alcohol

Heavy drinking can lead to chronic pancreatitis and insulin resistance.

Medium Impact
Poor Sleep

Less than 6 hours of sleep affects glucose metabolism and hormone balance.

Medium Impact

Non-Modifiable Risk Factors

Age (45+ years)

Risk increases with age due to decreased insulin sensitivity and beta-cell function.

Age-Related
Family History

Having a parent or sibling with diabetes doubles your risk.

Genetic
Ethnicity

Higher risk in African American, Hispanic, Native American, and Asian populations.

Genetic
Gestational Diabetes History

Previous gestational diabetes increases future type 2 diabetes risk by 50%.

Medical History

Healthy Lifestyle Choices

Discover practical lifestyle changes that can prevent diabetes and improve overall health through evidence-based strategies.

Nutrition Guidelines

Carbohydrate Management
  • Choose complex carbs over simple sugars
  • Aim for 45-65% of calories from carbs
  • Use the plate method: 1/4 plate starchy foods
  • Read nutrition labels for hidden sugars
Protein Intake
  • Include protein at every meal
  • Choose lean sources: fish, poultry, beans
  • Aim for 0.8-1g per kg body weight
  • Plant proteins help reduce diabetes risk
Healthy Fats
  • Focus on omega-3 fatty acids
  • Use olive oil and avocado oil
  • Include nuts and seeds daily
  • Limit saturated and trans fats
Meal Timing
  • Eat regular, consistent meals
  • Don't skip breakfast
  • Consider smaller, frequent meals
  • Stop eating 2-3 hours before bed

Exercise Recommendations

Aerobic Exercise

Improves insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake

  • 150 minutes moderate intensity per week
  • Or 75 minutes vigorous intensity
  • Examples: brisk walking, swimming, cycling
  • Start with 10-minute sessions
Resistance Training

Builds muscle mass and improves glucose metabolism

  • 2+ days per week, all major muscle groups
  • 8-12 repetitions, 2-3 sets each exercise
  • Use weights, resistance bands, or body weight
  • Allow 48 hours recovery between sessions
Flexibility & Balance

Supports overall fitness and prevents injuries

  • Daily stretching and flexibility work
  • Yoga or tai chi 2-3 times per week
  • Balance exercises for older adults
  • Focus on major muscle groups

Stress Management

Chronic stress can raise blood glucose levels and increase diabetes risk. Effective stress management is crucial for prevention.

Mindfulness & Meditation

10-15 minutes daily can reduce stress hormones and improve glucose control.

Quality Sleep

7-9 hours per night. Poor sleep affects glucose metabolism and increases diabetes risk.

Social Support

Strong relationships and social connections help manage stress and improve health outcomes.

Time Management

Organize priorities, set realistic goals, and maintain work-life balance.

Health Monitoring Guidelines

Learn when and how to monitor your health parameters for early diabetes detection and ongoing health maintenance.

Blood Glucose Testing

Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)
Normal: <100 mg/dL
Prediabetes: 100-125 mg/dL
Diabetes: ≥126 mg/dL

Frequency: Every 3 years if normal, annually if prediabetic

Hemoglobin A1C (HbA1c)
Normal: <5.7%
Prediabetes: 5.7-6.4%
Diabetes: ≥6.5%

Frequency: Every 3 years if normal, every 1-2 years if at risk

Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)
Normal: <140 mg/dL
Prediabetes: 140-199 mg/dL
Diabetes: ≥200 mg/dL

When: Used when other tests are borderline or for gestational diabetes screening

Physical Measurements

Body Mass Index (BMI)
Normal: 18.5-24.9
Overweight: 25.0-29.9
Obese: ≥30.0

Frequency: Monthly self-monitoring, annually by healthcare provider

Waist Circumference
Men: <40 inches (102 cm)
Women: <35 inches (88 cm)

Significance: Abdominal obesity is strongly linked to diabetes risk

Blood Pressure
Normal: <120/80 mmHg
Elevated: 120-129/<80
High: ≥130/80 mmHg

Frequency: At least every 2 years if normal, more often if elevated

Screening Schedule

Age 35+
Initial Screening

Begin regular diabetes screening with FPG or HbA1c for all adults

High Risk
Annual Screening

If BMI ≥25 plus additional risk factors, or previous prediabetes

Low Risk
Every 3 Years

If previous tests normal and no new risk factors developed

Pregnancy
Gestational Screening

Screen at 24-28 weeks, earlier if risk factors present

Self-Monitoring Tips
  • Keep a health journal with key measurements
  • Track weight, blood pressure, and physical activity
  • Note symptoms like increased thirst, urination, or fatigue
  • Schedule regular check-ups with healthcare providers
  • Discuss family history and personal risk factors
